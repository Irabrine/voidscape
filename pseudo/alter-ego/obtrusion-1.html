On the rooftop of one large elegant restaurant, sits a strong young man, with a feminine face and delicate features and long combed hair wearing a tracksuit, and a young purple-haired woman, wearing a red dress, sitting by the edge looking out from their table at a garden nearby.
The woman elegantly puts the fork with the cut piece of steak into her mouth.
“You sure she’ll get through this Nero?”
She speaks with a light-to-the-ear voice.
“She’ll do just fine, the question is if she would accept us afterwards or not.”
The man speaks nonchalantly as he’s eating from his bowl of lentils.
“I still think the operation choice was hastily made, we could’ve smoothed it over some more.”
“I have complete faith in her, plus, if things go south it just means she wasn’t good enough.”
“But a good person will die, possibly more if she’s not careful.”
The man shrugs without answering.

“So, her group managed to get into an Observer Outpost’s possible attenders.”
The pity in her voice disappeared completely along with the previous topic.
“Honestly, I didn’t expect that.”
He voices his surprise with the lowest degree of enthusiasm but just enough for it to count as a surprise.
“Was it Sara that influenced it?”
The genuine interest put in that question can be heard.
“As far as I can tell, that wasn’t her doing, the entire collective somehow reached a state of individuality and got its own factors.”
“Should we be worried? If she’s a part of the collective that’ll affect her luck.”
“Sadly not, she cursed with invariable luck.”
A sad expression forms on the woman’s face while the man’s doesn’t change during the whole conversation.
“She could’ve had a normal life.”
“Sometimes the world has its own plans for you.”
Silence is shared between the two as they finish their meals.

“Well, I’m off to the casino, drop by later if you like.”
The woman says this as she stands on the ledge of the building.
“Don’t stay out too late, it’s movie night.”
The man answers with a sad smile on his face.
“I wouldn’t miss it for the world.”
The woman says so with a big smile on her face, she then takes a step forward disappearing as she falls vertically.
Nobody else eating on the top floor seems to have noticed the woman who jumped off.
The man heaves a miserable sigh and traces a yellow-striped motorcycle that drives off in the distance with his ever tired eyes.
After finishing his drink, the man slowly walks downstairs where he pays the waiter and leaves the restaurant.

As he walks through the street, he gets a phone call. He listens, and the bored expression on his face changes into surprise, nay, horror.
“I’m sorry, WHAT?!”
He stops in his tracks for a good minute before hanging up the phone and quickly calls someone else on his contacts.
The other side picks up almost instantly.
“Yes?”
The voice from the other side is the same as the woman he was having dinner with.
“We have an emergency, I’m going to drop off Sara’s package, meet me at the warehouse in 13.”
The man’s face changes to a slight smirk of excitement.
“I haven’t heard you this excited in years.”
“The cat’s back!”
